## Revision log — what changed and why

1. **"Technology — Complex Technical Solutions" replaced with two source-verified cards** ("Manufacturing & Industrial — Equipment & Machinery Service Requests" and "Consumer Electronics — Appliance Warranty Claims"). The original card used generic, unsourced language ("complex technical equipment"). The replacement language is sourced directly from the ETA and TOS Varnsdorf customer journey map transcripts — TOS's own term is "service request," not "claim"; ETA's own highest-priority insight is pre-claim deflection, not generic warranty processing. This also aligns the Featured section 1:1 with the homepage's "Industrial Equipment & Machinery Service" and "Consumer Electronics & Appliance Warranty" labels — same terminology, same framing, on both pages.

2. **"Pharma Distribution Claims" renamed to "Pharma Cold Chain & Distribution Claims" and moved out of Featured into the More Use Cases table.** The original framing under "Healthcare & Pharma" implied this represented pharma claims broadly. It only covers cold-chain logistics — a real but narrow slice of pharma. It does not represent product complaints, pharmacovigilance, or Patient Access/Hub Services, none of which are confirmed enough to publish yet (Bayer scenario unresolved — internal team vs. hub provider model still needs confirmation). Demoting this card avoids overstating pharma coverage while keeping the case visible.

3. **"Health Insurance Claims" description and capability cues rewritten** to match the homepage Health Insurance label exactly ("Prior authorization adjudication," "Coverage determination documentation") instead of the original "Medical coding validation; Pre-authorization checks." Same underlying use case, now identical wording across both pages so a visitor clicking through from homepage to use-cases doesn't see a mismatched description of the same thing.

4. **"Banking Card Claims" renamed to "Banking Card Disputes & Chargebacks"** and capability cues updated to include "Chargeback evidence assembly & deadline tracking" — matching the homepage Banking & Fintech label's verified terminology (chargeback and dispute are related but distinct concepts; the card previously didn't name chargebacks explicitly despite describing chargeback processing in the body text).

5. **"Industrial Equipment Claims" (Manufacturing, in the More Use Cases table) capability cue updated** from "Maintenance log review" to "Technician workload tracking" to avoid duplicating language from the now-separate Featured card and to reflect the TOS-sourced insight about workload visibility.

6. **Card count label changed from a fixed number to "Showing all use cases."** The original "Showing 24 of 24" required manual updates every time a card was added or removed — a maintenance burden with no real benefit, since the page has no filter yet that would make showing a partial count meaningful. No cards were added or removed in this revision; content was revised in place.

7. **Added a new "Pharma Product Complaint Handling" card to the More Use Cases table**, placed directly after "Pharma Cold Chain & Distribution Claims" so both pharma-related cards sit together. This mirrors the homepage's cautious Pharma label (Variant A, 2 bullets, product-complaint terminology only) — same scope, same restraint. It deliberately does NOT include Patient Access / Hub Services / benefits verification / appeal letter language, since the Bayer scenario (internal team vs. external hub provider) remains unconfirmed and that terminology is structurally US-specific, which doesn't fit a primarily European market. Because the inventory label was changed to "Showing all use cases" (point 6 above), adding this card required no count adjustment elsewhere on the page.
